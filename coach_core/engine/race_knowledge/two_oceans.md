# Two Oceans Ultra Marathon — Race Intelligence

## Overview
- Distance: 56 km
- Route: Cape Town — Muizenberg — Chapman's Peak — Constantia Nek — finish at UCT/Green Point (verify current year)
- Date: Easter Saturday, April
- Cut-off: 7 hours (gun to gun)
- Elevation gain: ~1,100 m
- Character: "The Most Beautiful Race in the World." Four significant climbs after the 21 km mark. The back half is where Two Oceans either rewards or destroys you.

## Course Profile

| Segment | Distance | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Start — Muizenberg | 0–21 km | Flat. Deceptively comfortable. Too many go out too fast here. |
| Muizenberg — Sun Valley | 21–28 km | First real hills. Short sharp climbs. Legs warm up. |
| Chapman's Peak | 28–35 km | The signature climb. 4 km of relentless ascent. Run/walk here. |
| Noordhoek — Kommetjie | 35–42 km | Rolling coastal. Reward after Chapman's. |
| Constantia Nek | 42–47 km | Second major climb. This is where the race is decided. Comes when legs are already compromised. |
| Constantia — Finish | 47–56 km | Mostly downhill but quads are wrecked. Road is technical. |

## Key Hills (In Order)
1. **Ou Kaapse Weg** (~23 km) — Not the hardest but a wake-up call.
2. **Chapman's Peak** (~28–32 km) — The iconic climb. Exposed, beautiful, lung-burning. Walk it.
3. **Constantia Nek** (~43–46 km) — The race breaker. At 43 km on tired legs it feels twice as big as Chapman's.
4. **Final rollers** (~50–54 km) — Small but savage. Legs are empty by now.

## Pacing Strategy
1. **The flat first 21 km is a trap.** It feels like a half marathon. It is the first quarter of an ultra. Run it at ultra pace, not half marathon pace — typically 30-45 seconds/km slower than your half marathon race pace.
2. **Walk Chapman's Peak.** No exceptions below ~VDOT 52. Power-hiking saves your quads for Constantia Nek.
3. **Constantia Nek is where Two Oceans is won or lost.** Athletes who ran the first 40 km sensibly climb this with something left. Athletes who pushed Chapman's crawl up Constantia.
4. **The last 10 km goes downhill but feels uphill.** Quads fail on the descents if you overcook the climbs.
5. **Target pace:** Marathon pace +15–20% for the first 35 km, then effort-based.

## Nutrition Strategy
- Tables every 2–3 km from around 10 km.
- Water, Energade/PowerAde, Coke (from around 30 km), bananas, oranges.
- Eat real food on the climbs — stomach handles solids better at lower intensity.
- Take Coke on Constantia Nek. The caffeine + sugar hit helps enormously at 43 km.
- Gel every 40-45 min for the first half, every 30-35 min in the second half when effort increases.
- Salt: April in Cape Town can be cool but wind and effort still drain sodium. Take electrolytes every hour.

## Weather
- April in Cape Town: highly variable. 12-22°C typical. The south-easter (Cape Doctor) can make it feel much colder or create strong headwinds on exposed sections (Chapman's Peak cliff road is notorious for wind).
- Rain is possible. Hypothermia risk in extreme cases on Chapman's Peak if athlete slows significantly in cold and wind.
- Ideal race day: overcast, 16°C, no wind. Often not what you get.
- Hot years are rare but brutal — heat index on the final descent into the finish can catch runners off guard.

## Gear & Clothing
- Layer for the start (cool at 6 AM), plan to warm up by Chapman's Peak.
- Trail shoes optional — road shoes are fine for the full route, road surface throughout.
- Light arm warmers are useful. They pack small and save you if the south-easter arrives.
- Anti-chafe everything. 56 km in warm conditions or wet weather means hours of rubbing.

## Common Mistakes
- Running the first flat 21 km too fast (by far the most common error)
- Racing Chapman's Peak rather than hiking it
- Neglecting nutrition in the second half because effort goes up
- Underestimating Constantia Nek — it is brutal at 43 km regardless of how fit you are
- Sitting down in the medical tent at any point past 40 km

## Logistics
- Gun start: 6:00 AM (verify current year)
- Qualifier required: A certified marathon within a specified time window. Check Two Oceans Athletics Association for current year rules.
- Expo: Thursday-Friday at CTICC before race day.
- Parking: Very limited near the start. Most athletes use shuttles or stay close to the start.
- The finish area is electric — one of the best atmospheres in SA sport.
