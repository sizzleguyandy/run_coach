# Comrades Marathon — Race Intelligence

## Overview
- Distance: ~90 km (alternates direction each year)
- Route: Durban ↔ Pietermaritzburg, KwaZulu-Natal
- Date: Youth Day weekend, June
- Cut-off: 12 hours (gun to gun)
- Elevation: Up Run ~1,860 m gain / Down Run ~1,860 m descent
- Character: The world's oldest and largest ultra marathon. "The Ultimate Human Race."

## Direction — This Changes Everything
The course flips every year. Always confirm the current year's direction.

**Up Run (Durban → PMB):** Starts flat along the beachfront, then relentlessly climbs. The bulk of the gain comes in the second half. Harder physically but mentally easier — you know you're going up.

**Down Run (PMB → Durban):** Starts with a brutal 7 km downhill from PMB. Quads are the limiting factor. More technically demanding. The famous "little Polly's" descent and the 45th Cutting appear in the back half.

## Pacing Strategy
Comrades cannot be paced like a road marathon. The key rules:

1. **Start conservatively — always.** The Durban start (Up Run) draws athletes out too fast. The first 20 km feel easy. They will punish you at 70 km.
2. **Use effort, not pace.** Hills, heat, and fatigue make pace meaningless. Run by feel — a comfortable conversational effort for the first 60 km.
3. **Walk the big hills.** Fast walking on steep climbs is faster overall than running them and blowing up. Every Comrades legend walks Fields Hill, Inchanga, Polly Shortts.
4. **Protect the quads on Down Run.** Brake with your glutes, not your quads. Shorten your stride on descents. If quads blow at 40 km on a Down Run, the back half is miserable.
5. **The back 30 km is the race.** The first 60 km is just surviving until the real race starts.

## Calculating Your Comrades Pace From VDOT
A rough ultra pace predictor for Comrades based on marathon equivalent:
- Comrades finishing time ≈ marathon time × 2.2 to 2.4 (Up Run) or 2.1 to 2.3 (Down Run)
- Add 10-15% for a hot or humid year
- Inexperienced ultra runners should add 10% to their calculated time

For checkpoint planning: divide expected finish time across the segments proportionally, adding 5-8% extra time for the last 20 km.

## Nutrition Strategy
- **First 30 km:** Stick to your tested fuel. Gels, bars, or whatever you've trained with. Nothing new on race day.
- **Comrades tables:** Every ~3 km. Water, Energade (sports drink), Coke (from ~50 km), bananas, potatoes with salt, Vaseline.
- **Coke strategy:** Many Comrades runners swear by Coke from halfway. The combination of sugar, caffeine, and sodium works well for ultra efforts.
- **Ultra Mel:** Custard available at certain tables in later stages. A Comrades tradition — some athletes swear by it.
- **Sodium:** You will lose enormous amounts of salt. Take salt sachets or tablets every 45-60 min from early on. Cramps at 70 km are a sodium problem, not a hydration problem.
- **Never skip a table.** From 50 km onwards, drink at every table even if you don't feel thirsty.

## Gear & Clothing
- **Shoes:** Use well-broken-in shoes (50-100 km on them). Ultra cushion is fine but not mandatory. Never race in new shoes.
- **Socks:** Anti-blister socks. Double-layer or wool blend. Change socks at halfway if you can.
- **Lubrication:** Bodyglide or Vaseline everything. Nipples, inner thighs, underarms, toes. At 80 km, chafing becomes agony.
- **June weather:** Durban can be warm and humid even in June (18-25°C). Dress for running, not standing at 5 AM.
- **Drop bags:** You can submit a drop bag at designated points. Use it for a fresh pair of socks, extra gels, and a backup headlamp (early starters).

## Weather
- June in KZN: typically 8-15°C in PMB at the start, 18-25°C in Durban (Down Run) or building to 20-28°C in PMB by finish (Up Run).
- Humidity is the enemy, particularly on the Up Run. Sweat doesn't cool you when it can't evaporate.
- Heat index often makes it feel 4-6°C warmer than air temperature.
- Slow your target pace by 15-20 seconds/km for every 5°C above 20°C.

## Common Mistakes
- Going out too fast in the first 20 km (the most common cause of DNF or Vic Clapham finish)
- Under-fuelling in the first half ("I still feel good")
- Skipping salt / electrolytes early
- New shoes on race day
- Sitting down at a table after 60 km — very hard to get moving again
- Ignoring blisters until they become debilitating
- Trying to make up time on the climbs

## Logistics
- Gun start: 5:30 AM (verify each year)
- Seedings: A-H based on qualifier and previous results. Start with your correct seeding — running ahead causes DQ risk.
- Qualifier: Must complete a certified marathon under a qualifying standard within a specified window. Check current year rules.
- Numbers must be collected from Expo (usually Wednesday-Friday before the race).
- Book accommodation at the finish city well in advance — it sells out months ahead.
