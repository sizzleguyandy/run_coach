# Comrades Marathon

## The Course
The Comrades Marathon is a point-to-point ultra-marathon run annually between the cities of Durban and Pietermaritzburg in KwaZulu-Natal. The direction alternates each year between the "Up" run (from Durban) and the "Down" run (from Pietermaritzburg). It is the world's oldest and largest ultra-marathon, known for its grueling hills and the famous "Big Five" climbs. The total elevation gain is approximately **1800 metres**, categorised as high hilliness.

## Key Climbs / Challenges
- **~0-20 km — Cowies Hill & Fields Hill (Up Run):** The first major tests, with steep gradients that can crush early enthusiasm.
- **~30-50 km — Inchanga & Polly Shortts:** The toughest middle section, featuring relentless undulations and the infamous Polly Shortts climb, a soul-destroying ascent near the end of the Up run.
- **General Challenge:** Managing energy across the severe elevation gain. The Down run, while net downhill, is equally brutal on the quads and requires specific muscular endurance.

## Aid Stations
Aid stations are positioned approximately every 2-3 km and provide water and Coca-Cola (the official sponsor). Later stations may offer boiled potatoes and energy drinks. The race is famous for its "seconding" system, where personal support crews can hand up supplies at designated points.

## Pacing Advice
Even pacing is a myth here. Run to effort, especially in the first half. For the Up run, conserve energy relentlessly on the climbs, power-walking the steepest sections. For the Down run, hold back significantly on the descents to save your quads for the relentless later climbs.

## Gear Notes
Road shoes are standard, but a well-cushioned pair is advisable for the extensive tarmac pounding. Hydration packs or belts are common due to the distance. A hat and sunscreen are essential for the June winter sun.

## Tips for TR3D Runners
This is the ultimate test of endurance. Your training must prioritize long runs on hilly terrain to build muscular resistance. Use the power-walk on steep climbs to keep your effort at or below Threshold (T). The mental game is paramount; practice breaking the race into the "Big Five" hills and focus only on reaching the next one.
