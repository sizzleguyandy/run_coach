# Durban International Marathon

## The Course
A flat and fast marathon starting at the old Durban International Airport in Prospecton and finishing at the People's Park next to Moses Mabhida Stadium. The course is a loop through Durban's central and northern suburbs on wide, smooth tarmac roads with minimal turns. It is designed for speed and has already produced some of the fastest times on South African soil. Total elevation gain is just **30 metres**, making it one of the flattest marathon courses in the world.

## Key Climbs / Challenges
- This is one of the flattest marathon courses in the world, with minimal elevation change. The primary challenge is dealing with potential heat and humidity, as Durban has a subtropical climate.

## Aid Stations
Aid stations are placed at regular intervals, providing water and sports drinks. The route is fully closed to traffic, offering a safe and unobstructed path for runners.

## Pacing Advice
This is an ideal course for a negative split. Use the first half to settle into a comfortable rhythm and then gradually increase your pace. The flat terrain makes it easy to lock into your goal marathon pace and maintain it with high efficiency.

## Gear Notes
Lightweight racing flats are perfect for this fast course. Wear light, breathable clothing to manage the humid conditions. A hat or visor is recommended for sun protection.

## Tips for TR3D Runners
This course is a pure test of your aerobic engine. Without hills to break up the rhythm, your ability to hold a steady Threshold (T) pace is paramount. Practice long, steady-state runs at marathon pace to prepare both your body and mind for the monotony of a completely flat course.
