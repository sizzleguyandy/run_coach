# African Bank Soweto Marathon

## The Course
Known as "The People's Race", this marathon starts and finishes at the Nasrec Expo Centre, near the iconic FNB Stadium. The route winds through the historic streets of Soweto, passing significant landmarks like Vilakazi Street — the only street in the world to have been home to two Nobel Peace Prize laureates, Nelson Mandela and Desmond Tutu. Total elevation gain is approximately **300 metres**.

## Key Climbs / Challenges
- **~17 km — Vilakazi Street Climb:** A short but sharp ascent through the heart of Soweto, famous for its incredible crowd support.
- **~35 km — Orlando Stadium Climb:** A challenging late-race hill that tests tired legs.
- **General Challenge:** The high altitude of Johannesburg (around 1,700m) is the invisible factor that makes this race deceptively difficult, even with modest elevation gain.

## Aid Stations
Aid stations are frequent and well-stocked with water and energy drinks, often supported by vibrant local community groups. The on-course entertainment is a major feature, with music and dancing at almost every corner.

## Pacing Advice
Due to the altitude, it is crucial to adjust your goal pace. Expect to run 10-15 seconds per kilometer slower than your sea-level marathon pace. Aim for an even effort rather than even pace. Be especially conservative on the early inclines to save energy for the late-race climb at Orlando Stadium.

## Gear Notes
Standard road racing shoes are fine. A hat and sunglasses are essential, as the course can be exposed to the high-altitude African sun. Stay hydrated, as the dry air can accelerate fluid loss.

## Tips for TR3D Runners
Focus on running by perceived effort rather than pace. Your Threshold (T) pace at sea level will feel much harder at altitude. Use this race as a lesson in running at a lower percentage of your VO2max while maintaining a strong sense of form and rhythm.
