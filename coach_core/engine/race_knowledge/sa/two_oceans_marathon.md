# Two Oceans Marathon

## The Course
This 56km ultra-marathon is a stunning circular route starting and finishing near the University of Cape Town. It takes runners south along the False Bay coastline, over the iconic Chapman's Peak Drive, through the picturesque Hout Bay, and back over Constantia Nek. The course is a constant mix of breathtaking coastal views and challenging, rolling terrain. Total elevation gain is approximately **747 metres**.

## Key Climbs / Challenges
- **~15-22 km — Chapman's Peak:** The signature climb of the race. A steady ascent gaining around 200m over 5km, with the reward of spectacular ocean views at the summit.
- **~42-48 km — Constantia Nek:** A long, grinding climb that arrives late in the race, just after the official marathon mark. This is where many a runner's legs have given out.
- **General Challenge:** The total elevation gain is deceptively tough, compounded by the fact that it all comes in the second half of the race.

## Aid Stations
Aid stations are located every 3-4 km, well-stocked with water and energy drinks (Coca-Cola). Support is excellent, with enthusiastic local crowds and music adding to the vibrant atmosphere.

## Pacing Advice
Hold back for the first 30km along the relatively flat False Bay coastline. This is not a race to bank time; it's a race to bank energy. Prepare to shift to a disciplined, climbing pace for the two major climbs, and use the flatter sections to recover and hydrate.

## Gear Notes
Standard road racing flats are suitable. Dress in layers as the weather can be variable, with a cool coastal start giving way to warmer inland temperatures. A GPS watch with an elevation profile is highly recommended.

## Tips for TR3D Runners
Treat this as a marathon with a 14km hilly add-on. Your marathon training will serve you well for the first 42km. From there, it's about managing your Threshold (T) effort on the final climb up Constantia Nek. Practice long runs with a significant climb at the 30-35km mark to simulate the specific fatigue you'll face.
