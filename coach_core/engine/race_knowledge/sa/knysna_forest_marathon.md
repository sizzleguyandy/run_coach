# Knysna Forest Marathon

## The Course
A unique and challenging trail marathon set entirely within the ancient, mystical Knysna Forest in the Western Cape. The route is a point-to-point journey on a mix of jeep track and single-track trails, winding through a dense canopy of indigenous trees, across bubbling streams, and over the rugged terrain of the forest floor. Total elevation gain is approximately **700 metres**, categorised as medium hilliness.

## Key Climbs / Challenges
- **~5-15 km — Initial Forest Climbs:** A series of steep, technical climbs that quickly sap leg strength on the soft forest floor.
- **~25-35 km — The "Honeymoon Valley" Section:** A long, gradual descent followed by a brutal, steep climb out of the valley, testing both quads and lungs.
- **General Challenge:** The total elevation gain is significant, but the real challenge is the uneven, technical terrain and the humid, energy-sapping environment under the forest canopy.

## Aid Stations
Aid stations are more spaced out than in a city marathon due to the remote nature of the course. They typically provide water, energy drinks, and basic snacks. Runners are strongly advised to carry their own hydration.

## Pacing Advice
Pacing is dictated by the terrain, not a watch. The focus should be on managing effort on the climbs and recovering on the downhills. Power-hiking the steepest sections is not a sign of weakness but an essential strategy for finishing strong. Do not expect to run a personal best time here.

## Gear Notes
Trail running shoes with aggressive grip are mandatory. A hydration pack or belt with at least 500ml of water capacity is essential, as aid stations can be up to 8-10km apart. Gaiters are also recommended to keep out small stones and forest debris.

## Tips for TR3D Runners
This race is an excellent test of your overall running fitness and strength. Your threshold pace is irrelevant; the goal is to find a sustainable, rhythmic effort on the climbs, even if that means hiking. Focus on your form and foot placement on the technical descents to protect your quads and prevent a fall.
