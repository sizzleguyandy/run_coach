# parkrun — Race Intelligence

## Overview
- Distance: 5.0 km
- Format: Free, weekly, timed 5K runs held every Saturday at 8:00 AM at hundreds of venues across South Africa and worldwide
- Cut-off: None — everyone is welcome at every pace
- Elevation: Varies by venue (see below)
- Character: Not a race — a community event. Walkers, joggers, and serious runners all participate. A fantastic weekly fitness benchmark and social run.

## How parkrun Works
- Free to participate — register once at parkrun.com, get your barcode, bring it every time.
- Results published within a few hours of the event.
- Volunteer-run — the community that runs together runs together.
- Dog-friendly at most venues.

## SA parkrun Venues — Selected Profiles

### Rondebosch Common (Cape Town)
- Surface: Grass/gravel
- Profile: Mostly flat with a gentle rise. One of Cape Town's fastest courses.
- Wind: Can be exposed to the south-easter.

### Green Point (Cape Town)
- Surface: Paved path
- Profile: Flat. Sea Point promenade area. Fast course.
- Wind: Sea breeze / south-easter exposure.

### Delta Park (Johannesburg)
- Surface: Mixed trail/grass
- Profile: Gently rolling. Not a PB course but beautiful.
- Altitude: ~1,750 m — see altitude note.

### Marks Park (Johannesburg)
- Surface: Grass / packed gravel
- Profile: Mostly flat. Popular Joburg course.
- Altitude: ~1,750 m.

### Hobie Beach (Port Elizabeth / Gqeberha)
- Surface: Beach path / paved
- Profile: Flat. One of SA's fastest parkruns.
- Wind: Exposed to coastal wind.

### Albert Luthuli (Durban)
- Surface: Paved park paths
- Profile: Flat to gently rolling.
- Humidity: High year-round. Heat and humidity in summer add significantly to effort.

## Using parkrun for Training

### As a Benchmark
- A 5K parkrun time is the foundation for VDOT calculation.
- **Best conditions for a benchmark time trial:** Flat course, cool morning, fresh legs (no hard session Wednesday or Thursday).
- Run at maximum effort — start slightly conservatively (first km not your fastest), build through km 2–3, hold on through km 4–5.
- Submit your time to your coach/bot to recalculate paces and update your training zones.

### As an Easy Run
- Run parkrun at easy pace (2–3 min/km slower than your 5K PB pace).
- Excellent for recovery weeks — the atmosphere makes it enjoyable.

### As a Tempo Run
- Run the first 2 km easy, middle km at threshold, final 2 km at race effort.
- Useful mid-training-cycle fitness check without full race stress.

## Pacing the 5K

### For a PB Attempt
- First km: Goal pace or 3–5 sec/km slower.
- Km 2–4: Lock onto goal pace. This is where the race is won.
- Km 5: Whatever you have left.
- Positive splits are common but negative splitting (going faster in the second half) is more efficient — aim for even splits as a minimum.

### Heart Rate Guidance for 5K
- 5K is primarily anaerobic at the end — expect 90–95% max HR by km 3–4.
- If you hit 95% at km 1, you started too fast.

## Altitude (Gauteng Venues)
- Johannesburg parkruns sit at ~1,750 m above sea level.
- Sea-level runners should expect 5–8% slower times at altitude.
- Example: a 25:00 sea-level 5K runner might run 26:15–27:00 at a Joburg parkrun.
- Use the altitude-adjusted time (not the raw time) to calculate VDOT if you train at sea level.

## parkrun Tourism
- SA has hundreds of venues. Travelling? Find your nearest parkrun at parkrun.co.za.
- Complete parkruns at different venues to collect "tourism" milestones.

## Milestones
- 10 runs, 25, 50, 100, 250, 500 — special T-shirts awarded at 50 and 100 runs.
- Volunteer milestones also recognised (25 volunteering events = volunteer T-shirt).

## Common Mistakes
- Starting too fast (km 1 at PB pace when you haven't warmed up)
- Using parkrun as a race every single week — undermines training periodisation
- Not warming up before a PB attempt — at least 5–10 minutes of easy jogging
- Forgetting your barcode — you still ran, but you won't get a result without scanning

## Logistics
- Start: Every Saturday at 8:00 AM sharp
- Registration: Once only, online at parkrun.com — free
- Barcode: Print or show on your phone
- Course briefing: 7:55 AM for newcomers — attend your first time
- Results: Available on the parkrun website within a few hours
