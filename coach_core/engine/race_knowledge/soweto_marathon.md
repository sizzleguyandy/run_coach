# Soweto Marathon — Race Intelligence

## Overview
- Distance: 42.195 km
- Route: FNB Stadium, Soweto — loops through Soweto townships
- Date: First Sunday of November
- Cut-off: 6 hours
- Elevation gain: ~350 m (rolling, with several notable climbs)
- Character: One of Africa's great community marathons. Runs through the heart of Soweto — past Vilakazi Street, the Hector Pieterson Memorial, Kliptown. A deeply meaningful race with enormous crowd support.

## Course Profile

| Segment | Distance | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| FNB Stadium start | 0–5 km | Rolling out of the stadium precinct. Atmosphere electric. Don't go out too hard. |
| Orlando / Diepkloof | 5–15 km | Undulating township roads. Some short sharp climbs. |
| Vilakazi Street area | 15–22 km | Historic heart of Soweto. Crowd support excellent here. |
| Kliptown / Dobsonville | 22–33 km | Rolling. The quiet middle section. This is where races are decided. |
| Return to FNB Stadium | 33–42 km | Mix of road types. Final 5 km back to the stadium — emotionally powerful. |

## Key Features
- **The hills:** Unlike Cape Town marathon, Soweto has more frequent rolling hills throughout. No single monster climb but no flat sections either. Consistent effort management required.
- **Crowd support:** Outstanding through the township sections. The noise and energy can pull you faster than planned — be disciplined.
- **Road surfaces:** Mix of good tar and older township roads. Some sections have potholes or uneven surfaces — stay alert.
- **The finish at FNB Stadium:** Running into the stadium bowl is one of the most memorable experiences in SA road running.

## Pacing Strategy
1. **The early atmosphere is a trap.** The crowd noise around the stadium start pulls athletes out fast. Stay at goal pace.
2. **Respect the rolling hills.** Run effort, not pace. Allow pace to drop slightly on uphills rather than forcing it — you'll make it up on the downs.
3. **Even effort beats even pace here.** The undulations mean your splits will vary. A heart rate target (75–80% for the first 25 km) is more reliable than a pace target.
4. **The last 5 km is harder than it looks on paper.** The route back to the stadium has a few last hills. You'll be tired. Don't go through halfway faster than you can hold.

## Weather
- November in Gauteng: 18–28°C typical on race day. Start (7:00 AM) is cooler — 15–20°C — but heats up quickly.
- Low humidity compared to coastal races, but the altitude (1,750 m above sea level) is significant.
- **Altitude effect:** Soweto sits at ~1,750 m. If you train at sea level, adjust expectations by 2–3% or 60–90 seconds on marathon time. Your pace will feel harder than your watch suggests.
- Afternoon thunderstorms are possible in November (Highveld storm season) but gun time at 6:00–7:00 AM usually avoids them.
- Sun exposure: Highveld sun is intense even in spring. Use sunscreen.

## Altitude Guidance (Sea Level Runners)
- Add 4–6% to predicted finish time if you live at sea level and arrive within a week.
- Acclimatise for 10+ days or arrive within 24 hours of the race (better to race before full acclimatisation disruption).
- Paces will feel harder at threshold and above — use heart rate, not pace, for quality sessions.

## Nutrition
- Water and sports drink stations every 3 km.
- Gels provided at key points (check current year).
- Altitude increases fluid loss slightly — drink to schedule, not thirst.
- Take fuel at 10, 20, 30 km regardless of how you feel.

## Gear
- Standard road marathon gear.
- Light layer at the start if sub-15°C — you'll warm up by 10 km.
- Sunscreen is essential on the Highveld.

## Common Mistakes
- Going out too fast, carried by the stadium atmosphere
- Sea-level runners underestimating altitude — expecting sea-level paces
- Not drinking enough early (Highveld air is dry, sweat evaporates faster)
- Treating it as a flat course — the rolling hills accumulate

## Logistics
- Gun start: ~6:00 AM (verify current year)
- Start/Finish: FNB Stadium, Nasrec area
- Expo: Typically Thursday–Saturday at FNB Stadium or nearby venue
- Parking: Limited. Public transport and shuttle options widely used.
- Entry: Highly competitive — entries open and fill quickly, often months in advance.
- This race sells out. Register early.
