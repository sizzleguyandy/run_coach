# Cape Town Marathon — Race Intelligence

## Overview
- Distance: 42.195 km
- Route: V&A Waterfront → Sea Point → Green Point → De Waal Park → Southern Suburbs → finish at Green Point
- Date: Late September / early October (Sunday)
- Cut-off: 6 hours
- Elevation gain: ~250 m (rolling, not flat)
- Character: IAAF Gold Label race. Fast course, spectacular scenery, international field. One of SA's most prestigious road marathons.

## Course Profile

| Segment | Distance | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| V&A Waterfront start | 0–5 km | Flat through the harbour precinct. Fast and exciting. Don't get carried away. |
| Sea Point Promenade | 5–12 km | Flat coastal road. Table Mountain views. Wind exposure. |
| Green Point / Signal Hill turns | 12–18 km | Rolling. A few short sharp climbs. |
| De Waal Drive / Southern Suburbs | 18–30 km | Undulating. The quiet middle section where races are decided. |
| Return to Green Point | 30–42 km | Mostly downhill and flat. The last 5 km is a real test of what you've left. |

## Key Features
- **Sea Point promenade section** (~6–11 km): Beautiful but exposed to the south-easter. Wind can add significant effort — felt as a tailwind going out and a punishing headwind coming back.
- **De Waal Drive (~22–27 km):** Rolling highway section. Mentally quiet. Stick to your pace here rather than trying to bank time.
- **The last 5 km:** Flat to slight downhill back to Green Point stadium. Where you find out if you paced the first half correctly.

## Pacing Strategy
1. **Start at goal pace or 5–10 sec/km slower.** The Waterfront atmosphere creates an adrenaline surge — contain it.
2. **Wind awareness on the promenade.** If the south-easter is blowing, the outbound leg will feel easy (tailwind). Don't bank pace — you'll pay on the return.
3. **Even splits or negative split wins this course.** The rolling back half is very runnable. Athletes who go out controlled come home strong.
4. **Heart rate target:** Aim for 75–80% max HR for the first 25 km, allow 80–85% from 25–35 km, race feel for the final 7 km.

## Weather
- Late September in Cape Town: 14–24°C typical, highly variable.
- The south-easter (SE trade wind) is the defining weather factor. Can be 30+ km/h, which adds 20–40 sec/km to exposed sections.
- Morning start (7:00 AM) is usually the coolest part of the day — the promenade section stays cooler due to sea influence.
- Occasionally overcast and cool — ideal running conditions. Check forecast the night before.

## Nutrition
- Water and sports drink every 3 km on course.
- Gels provided at key tables (check current year sponsor).
- Eat/drink to a schedule, not thirst. Cape Town's mild temperatures mean athletes underestimate fluid loss.
- Take fuel at 10, 20, 30 km regardless of how you feel.

## Gear
- Road shoes, standard marathon gear.
- A light layer or arm warmers if it's cold at the 7 AM start — you'll be warm by 10 km but the start can be 12–14°C.
- Sunscreen. Table Mountain sun is deceptive even in spring.

## Common Mistakes
- Going out too fast from the V&A — the energy is electric and pace judgement goes out the window
- Underestimating the SE wind on the return promenade leg
- Neglecting nutrition on the "easy" coastal section
- Not checking the wind forecast — this race changes character completely depending on wind

## Logistics
- Gun start: ~7:00 AM
- Start: V&A Waterfront / Clock Tower area
- Expo: Usually Thursday-Saturday at the CTICC
- Parking: Extremely limited. Public transport (MyCiTi bus) or hotel within walking distance strongly recommended.
- International race — very well organised, large international field, competitive atmosphere.
