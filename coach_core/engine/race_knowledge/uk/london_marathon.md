# TCS London Marathon

## The Course
The race starts in Blackheath, heads east to Woolwich before turning west. The route passes the Cutty Sark in Greenwich, crosses the iconic Tower Bridge, loops around Canary Wharf, and follows the Thames past the Tower of London before finishing on The Mall in front of Buckingham Palace. The course has a total elevation gain of approximately **127 metres**, making it a relatively flat urban marathon.

## Key Climbs / Challenges
- ~4 km: A very steep downhill heading into Woolwich. Relax and let gravity do the work.
- ~35 km: A slight change to circumvent the cobblestone area of the Tower of London.
- General Challenge: Navigating crowded streets, especially in the early miles, and potential wind on the Embankment.

## Aid Stations
Water is available every 2 miles from mile 3, with Lucozade Sport at miles 5, 10, 15, 19, and 23. Lucozade Sport Gels are provided at miles 14 and 19.

## Pacing Advice
The course is fast and mostly flat, so even splitting is recommended. Avoid going out too fast on the downhill sections in the first 10k. Save energy for the final 12km, which can be mentally challenging.

## Gear Notes
Standard road racing flats or lightweight trainers are ideal. A GPS watch is recommended for pacing, and dressing in layers is wise due to variable April weather.

## Tips for TR3D Runners
Use the long, flat sections to lock into Threshold (T) pace. The few small inclines can be tackled at Marathon (M) pace without spiking your effort. Focus on maintaining form when the crowds are at their loudest.
