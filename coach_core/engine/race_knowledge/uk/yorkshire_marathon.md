# Yorkshire Marathon

## The Course
The race starts and finishes at the University of York. The route takes a clockwise loop through York's historic city centre, passing York Minster, and then heads out into the surrounding countryside of the Vale of York. The course is largely flat, with a total elevation gain of **157 metres**.

## Key Climbs / Challenges
- The course is largely flat with a few gentle undulations. The highest point is only around 40m above sea level.
- General Challenge: The flat, open countryside can be exposed to wind.

## Aid Stations
Aid stations are located throughout the course, providing water and sports drinks. Refer to the official race day guide for specific details.

## Pacing Advice
This is another course conducive to even or negative splitting. The flat terrain allows you to dial into your goal pace and maintain it with discipline.

## Gear Notes
Lightweight trainers or racing flats are ideal. Prepare for variable October weather with layers that can be discarded.

## Tips for TR3D Runners
Focus on maintaining a consistent Threshold (T) pace throughout. The flat course is perfect for practicing and executing a perfectly paced marathon. Pay attention to your form and efficiency.
