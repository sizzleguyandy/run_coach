# Baxters Loch Ness Marathon

## The Course
A scenic point-to-point race starting near Whitebridge on the south side of Loch Ness. It follows the loch's shore through Foyers, Inverfarigaig, and Dores, before crossing the River Ness and finishing in Bught Park, Inverness. This is the hilliest of the UK marathons featured here, with a total elevation gain of **349 metres**, categorised as medium hilliness.

## Key Climbs / Challenges
- ~0-10 km: A significant net downhill start, which can be hard on the quads.
- ~12-30 km: A middle "flat" section that is, in reality, rolling terrain.
- ~30 km: A drawn-out ascent leading to the final push into Inverness.
- General Challenge: The rural setting can be quiet with long stretches of road, requiring mental focus.

## Aid Stations
Aid stations with water and sports drinks are positioned along the route. Details are available in the official race handbook.

## Pacing Advice
Hold back significantly on the early downhill to save your legs. Aim for even effort on the rolling middle section, and be prepared for the final climb. Pacing by effort rather than pure time is crucial.

## Gear Notes
Road shoes are suitable. Due to the remote start and variable Scottish weather, bring layers and a foil blanket for the pre-race wait.

## Tips for TR3D Runners
This is a race for effort-based pacing. Use the early downhill to cruise at a comfortable Marathon (M) effort. On the climbs, keep your effort at or below Threshold (T) by shortening your stride. Practice running downhill efficiently in training to minimize quad damage.
