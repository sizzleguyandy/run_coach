# Brighton Marathon

## The Course
Starting at Preston Park, the course heads through the city centre past the Royal Pavilion, out to Ovingdean, and then back along the coast to a finish on Hove Lawns. The route features a total elevation gain of **145 metres**, with a notable climb early on and some rolling hills later.

## Key Climbs / Challenges
- ~1 km: An early climb up Preston Park Avenue, gaining 20m over 700m.
- ~22-25 km: The toughest hills come between miles 14 and 18, cresting at around 162m.
- General Challenge: Exposed coastal sections can be windy, and the atmosphere, while vibrant, can be distracting.

## Aid Stations
Aid stations with water and sports drinks are provided at regular intervals along the course. Check the official race guide for exact locations.

## Pacing Advice
Manage your effort on the early climb and the rolling section at miles 14-18. Use the flat coastal stretches to recover and maintain pace. A negative split is possible with smart pacing.

## Gear Notes
Standard road racing shoes are appropriate. Due to the coastal wind, a lightweight, breathable layer is recommended.

## Tips for TR3D Runners
Use the early and mid-race hills to practice your hill-running technique at Marathon (M) effort. The flatter sections are perfect for finding and holding your Threshold (T) pace. Focus on staying relaxed into the wind.
