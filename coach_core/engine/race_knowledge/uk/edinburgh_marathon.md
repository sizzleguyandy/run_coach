# Edinburgh Marathon

## The Course
The point-to-point route begins in the city centre, winds through Holyrood Park past Arthur's Seat, and heads east out of Edinburgh into East Lothian, finishing at the coast in Musselburgh. The course has a net downhill profile with a total elevation gain of just **38 metres**, making it one of the fastest marathons in the UK.

## Key Climbs / Challenges
- ~5-10 km: The route through Holyrood Park features some rolling terrain and the main climb past Arthur's Seat.
- General Challenge: The course is exposed along the coast, and wind can be a significant factor, especially in the later stages.

## Aid Stations
Aid stations are positioned along the course offering water and sports drinks. The exact frequency and brands are provided in the official race guide.

## Pacing Advice
Due to the net downhill profile, it's easy to start too fast. Aim for even pacing or a slight negative split. Conserve energy on the early climbs in Holyrood Park and use the flat coastal roads to maintain speed.

## Gear Notes
Road shoes are sufficient. Be prepared for potential coastal winds by wearing form-fitting clothing. A hat and sunglasses can be useful on the exposed sections.

## Tips for TR3D Runners
Treat the first 10k as a warm-up, running at the easy end of your Marathon (M) pace. Once you hit the flatter coastal sections, you can lock into Threshold (T) pace. Practice running in windy conditions during training.
