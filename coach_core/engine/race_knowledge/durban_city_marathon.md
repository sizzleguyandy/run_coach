# Durban City Marathon — Race Intelligence

## Overview
- Distance: 42.195 km
- Route: Central Durban loop — beachfront, Berea Ridge, return
- Date: June (traditionally the Sunday before Comrades)
- Cut-off: 6 hours
- Elevation gain: ~400 m (significant — includes the Berea Ridge climb)
- Character: One of SA's oldest marathons. Run the week before Comrades — many Comrades athletes use it as their final qualifier or a training run. Fast-ish course with a real climb. Durban humidity is the main challenge.

## Course Profile

| Segment | Distance | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Durban beachfront start | 0–8 km | Flat along the Golden Mile. Deceptively easy. Don't let pace drift. |
| Berea Ridge approach | 8–16 km | Gradual climb inland. The course rises steadily here. |
| Berea Ridge / Upper Berea | 16–26 km | The defining section. Rolling plateau with significant elevation. |
| Descent back to coast | 26–34 km | Quad-loading descent. Control your stride — you're not done yet. |
| Coastal return to finish | 34–42 km | Flat back along the beachfront. Last 3 km is typically fast with crowd support. |

## Key Features
- **The Berea Ridge (16–26 km):** Durban's distinctive topography. The city sits on a ridge above the beachfront — the marathon climbs it and comes back down. The climb is not brutal but it's sustained and comes when legs are already working.
- **Humidity:** Durban in June is 60–80% humidity. This is the most significant challenge of this race. Cooling is impaired, pace feels harder, and salt loss is extreme.
- **The beachfront finish:** Fast and flat with the ocean beside you. If you've run the first 35 km correctly, you'll enjoy this. If not, you'll be grinding.

## Durban as a Comrades Qualifier
- This race is traditionally used by Comrades runners as a last-chance qualifier.
- Running it as a Comrades qualifier: Run conservatively. Finish well within the qualifying standard. Your legs need to recover in 7 days.
- Running it as a Comrades warm-up: Run it at easy effort (marathon+30–40 sec/km slower than goal). It's a long training run, not a race.
- **Do NOT race the Durban City hard if Comrades is 7 days away.** The climb and humidity will cost you on race day.

## Pacing Strategy
1. **The flat first 8 km is an invite to go out too fast.** Resist. The Berea climb is coming.
2. **Treat the climb as an investment, not a loss.** Run it at easy effort — controlled HR, shorter stride, allow pace to drop. Athletes who fight the climb die on the descent.
3. **The descent (26–34 km) is where legs blow if you overcook the top.** Let gravity help but don't overstride. Quads pay for every reckless downhill.
4. **Humidity adjustment:** Add 20–30 sec/km to sea-level predictions for a humid June day.

## Weather
- June in Durban: 18–25°C on race morning. Humidity is the defining factor.
- Start (6:00 AM or 6:30 AM) is the coolest part of the day — but humidity is already high.
- By 9–10 AM, temperatures and humidity climb. Athletes who take longer than 4 hours will feel a significant heat build.
- No wind protection — beachfront sections are exposed.

## Altitude
- Sea level. No altitude adjustment needed. The Comrades course from Durban (Up Run) climbs to 750 m — athletes running Comrades should be aware their July Comrades will be warmer and more demanding than this race.

## Nutrition
- Water and sports drink every 2–3 km.
- Gels at key tables.
- **Electrolytes are critical.** June humidity in Durban means extreme salt loss. If you cramp in the back half, you needed more sodium from 10 km in.
- Coke available from approximately 25 km.

## Gear
- Lightweight, breathable kit. Heat and humidity demand it.
- Anti-chafe everything — salt-soaked skin chafes in humidity.

## Common Mistakes
- Racing hard the week before Comrades
- Going out too fast on the flat beachfront section
- Underestimating the Berea humidity compared to inland races
- Not taking electrolytes from the start

## Logistics
- Gun start: ~6:00 AM or 6:30 AM (verify current year)
- Start: Durban beachfront / City Hall area
- Expo: Friday–Saturday before race
- Parking: Available near the beachfront area but plan ahead
- This race is heavily used by Comrades runners — the field is large and competitive, with international Comrades athletes using it as a qualifier
